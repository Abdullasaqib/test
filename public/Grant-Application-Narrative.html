Building What's NEXT_: Democratizing AI Education for the World's Youth

Executive Summary

NEXT_ Billion Lab is on a mission to prepare 1 million young people for the AI economy by 2026. Through a research-backed, 12-week curriculum, students ages 9-16 learn to build real products using AI tools—transforming from technology consumers into technology creators. We seek funding to expand access to underserved communities globally.

Mission: Create job CREATORS, not job seekers. Prepare youth for jobs that don't exist yet by teaching them to BUILD with AI, not just use it.

The Problem: A Widening Opportunity Gap

Artificial intelligence represents the greatest economic opportunity in human history. By 2030, 85% of jobs will require AI fluency. Yet access to AI education is profoundly unequal:

  • A 14-year-old in Singapore has access to AI tools, mentorship, and entrepreneurship education.
  • A 14-year-old in rural Rwanda, urban Detroit, or remote India often does not.
  • Most schools worldwide are teaching students to USE technology, not CREATE with it.
  • Traditional coding education is becoming obsolete as AI tools enable anyone to build.

This gap will compound. Without intervention, the AI economy will exacerbate global inequality rather than reduce it.

Our Solution: NEXT_ Billion Lab

We have built a comprehensive, scalable solution that teaches youth to build with AI:

Curriculum Architecture

  • 12-Week Journey: 60 missions across 5 phases (Discover → Validate → Build → Launch → Pitch)
  • 1,100+ Micro-Sprints: 60-90 second learning units designed for young minds
  • 3 Age Tracks: Junior (9-11), Teen (12-14), Advanced (15-16)
  • 3 Certifications: AI Foundations, AI Builder, AI Launcher—all LinkedIn-shareable

Key Platform Features

  • AI Coach: 24/7 intelligent mentorship that guides without giving answers
  • THE TANK: Investor pitch simulation (like Shark Tank, but supportive)
  • Demo Day: Students present real products to family, peers, and community
  • Founder DNA: Assessment that identifies each student's unique strengths

Research Foundation

Our curriculum is built on established learning science frameworks:

  • Bloom's Taxonomy (every sprint is tagged by cognitive level)
  • Kolb's Experiential Learning Cycle
  • Growth Mindset Theory (Carol Dweck)
  • Zone of Proximal Development (Vygotsky)
  • Flow State Theory (Csikszentmihalyi)
  • Social Constructivism
